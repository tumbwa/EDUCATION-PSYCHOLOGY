BRANCHES OF PSYCHOLOGY

Every human has mind, and because psychology is the study of mind, where there are human being there is psychology. Based on problem and activities, we can categorize different branches of psychology , the follows are examples :-

Abnormal psychology

deals with psychopathology and abnormal behavior. It covers a broad range of disorders, from depression to obsession-compulsion to sexual deviation and many more.
Cognitive Psychology

Cognitive Psychology- studies mental processes including how people think, perceive, remember and learn.
Clinical and counseling psychology

Clinical and counseling psychology concerned with assessment and treatment of mental illness, abnormal behaviors and psychiatric problem
Forensic Psychology

Forensic Psychology also referred as Criminal psychology , this is the intersection of psychology and the law . It studies the role of Psychology in the legal systems and may serve as consultants and expert witnesses in the courtroom . Psychologists under this field are individuals who might be clinical psychologists, school psychologists, neurologists or counselors who lend their psychological expertise to provide testimony, analysis or recommendations in legal or criminal cases.
Industrial psychology

Industrial psychology: Applies psychological theories and principles to organization. It is also refer to as input and output psychology, focuses on increasing work place productivity and related issues such as physical and mental well being of employees. Performs a wide variety of tasks including studying workers attitudes and behaviors , evaluating companies and conduct leadership training.
Sport psychology

Sport psychology Studies how psychology influences sports, athletic performance, exercise and physical activity. Improve performance and increase motivation. Other professionals utilize exercise and sports to enhance people’s lives and well-being throughout the entire lifespan.
Developmental psychology

Developmental psychology is the branch of psychology that focuses on how people grow and change over the course of a lifetime. Study of all aspect of human growth i.e. Physical, emotional, intellectual, and perceptual and personality development. Developmental psychologists can work with people of all ages to treat issues and support growth, although some choose to specialize in a specific area such as childhood, adulthood, or old age
other branches

Other branches. Experimental psychology, comparative psychology cognitive psychology, sport psychology, social psychology and gender psychology..

Goals of psychology

We can simply understand goals of psychology by formula of 4w+h, these means the following :- What=what happened ?, Why happened?, Where = in what context can occur ?, When=prediction and How= how to perform,

  1. To predict behavior ,tell under what conditions is the behavior (event likely occur) (how)
  2. To control behavior –how is the principle applied or what change in condition is.
  3. To explain behavior, tell why. (why)
  4. To describe behavior, tell what occurred (what)
