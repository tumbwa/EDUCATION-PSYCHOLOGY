SIGMUD FREUD PERSONALITY THEORY

The father of depth psychology (also known as psycho dynamic or clinical psychology ) . Friends Psychoanalytic theory explains about three level of personality namely conscious, subconscious and unconscious where as a unconscious acts as determinants of personality. Psychoanalytic theory proposes that may deeply felt emotions and thoughts are found in the unconscious. The unconscious level contains the desire, wishes, needs and impulses that are not aware of under normal circumstance. Psychoanalysis as method for treating mental illness and theory which explains human behavior is often known as the talking cure.

Freud also stated that personality is composed of three systems that is Id, ego and super ego.
Id (Instinct)

Id (Instinct) self gratification: The Id is the original system of personality it is present at birth (nature)level according to the pleasure principle (gratification from satisfying basic instincts). Eros or life instincts help the individual to survive, it directs life sustaining activities such as respiration, eating, sex (Freud , 1925) the energy created by the life instincts is known as libido..
Ego (Reality).

Ego (Reality).  It involves learned ways of behaving; it plays a middle role between the desires of the Id and realities of outside word. The Ego develops from the Id during infancy .The goal is to satisfying the demands of the Id in a safe socially accepted way . it operates in both the conscious and unconscious mind. It includes the reason, judgment, planning, tolerance, memory, understanding, and so on.
Super Ego:

Refers to the moral aspects of the personality. It represents those values and ideas that are established and learned by individual . Our Ego tells us what is right and what is wrong it tries to prevent Id impulses from being expressed. Since most of Id impulses are socially unacceptable it evokes the feeling of guilt and shame (scolds or congratulated).

ERICK ERICKSON PERSONALITY DEVELOPMENT

Erick Erickson (1902 - 1982) was a German-American psychologist who together with his wife Joan, became known for his work on psychosocial development. He was influenced by Sigmund and Anna Freud and became famous for coining the phrase "identity crisis." Although Erikson lacked even a bachelor's degree, he served as a professor at Harvard and Yale. develop psychoanalytic theory of psychosocial development comprising eight stages from infancy to adulthood in each stage the individual meets and must solve a major crisis or problem . If the crisis is not solved the individual is less able to solve crises at later stages (however in later stage the person can solve an earlier crisis and continue developing favorably )

As people attempt to solve in each crisis, they learn ways of dealing with society.
Erick Erickson’s ideas were greatly influenced by Freud psychoanalytic , Erickson was an ego psychologist , he emphasized by the role of culture and society and the conflicts that can take place within the ego itself.
Erick Erickson maintained that personality develops in predetermined order and builds upon each previous stage. This called Epigenetic principle
according to the Erickson, successful completion of each stage results in a health personality and the acquisition of basic virtues. Basic virtues are characteristic strengths with the ego can use to resolve subsequent crises.

STAGES OF PERSONALITY

Every human has mind, and because psychology is the study of mind, where there are human being there is psychology. Based on problem and activities, we can categorize different branches of psychology , the follows are examples :-

Trust versus mistrust , Infancy (1-2 years):
stage one

This stage involves the crisis of establishing a feeling of basic trust or mistrust. As infants we ask ourselves if we can trust the world and we wonder if it's safe place or it is full of unpredictable events and accidents waiting to happen? . We learn that if we can trust someone now, we can also trust others in the future. If we experience fear, we develop doubt and mistrust. the infant at this stage is uncertain about the world in which live, to resolve these feelings of uncertainty, the infant looks towards their care giver (mother) for stability and consistence. The key to our development is our mother.
Autonomy versus shame and doubt, early childhood (2 – 3 years)
stage 2

In our early childhood, we experience ourselves and discover our body. We ask: is it okay to be me? If we are allowed to discover ourselves, then we develop self-confidence. If we are not, we can develop shame and self-doubt . also In this stage we strive for control over muscular activities including control of bladder. If such control is developed , children experience a feelings of autonomy and self governing . If not , we develop shame and doubt.. Both parents now play a major role
Initiative versus guilt, pre-school age (3 – 6 years):
stage 3

In preschool, we take initiative as he or she becomes in involved in activities and social relation. , try out new things, and learn basic principles like how round things roll. We ask: Is it okay for me to do what I do? If we are encouraged, we can follow our interests. also we tend to ask many questions as our charts for knowledge grows. If we are held back or told that what we do is silly, we can develop guilt. We are now learning from the entire family
Industry versus inferiority, school age (7 – 12 years ):
stage 4

Now we discover our own interests and realize that we are different from others. We want to show that we can do things right. We ask if we can make it in this world? If we receive recognition from our teachers or peers we become industrious, which is another word for hard-working we also acquire ability to learn. If we get too much negative feedback, we start to feel inferior and lose motivation. At this stages our peer group will gain greater significance and will become source of the self – esteem as we need to win approval. If initiative is not encouraged, if it’s restricted by parents or teacher, then the we begins to feel inferior. Doubt our own abilities and therefore may not reach potential. Our family neighbors and schools now influence us the most. the chance for our teacher is because teach the us specific skills
Identity vs. Role confusion , adolescent (13-20 yrs) )):
stage 5

During adolescence, we ask who we are? where are we going in life ? we learn that we have different social roles. We are friends, students, children and citizens. Many experience an identity crises. The stage between childhood and adulthood. Begin to look at the terms of career, relationship, family housing etc. Individual wants to belong to a society and fit in . If our parents now allow us to go out and explore, we can find identity. If they push us to conform to their views, we can face role confusion and feel lost. Key to our learning are our peers and role models.
Intimacy vs. Isolation , young adult (20-35) :
stage 6

As young adults we slowly understand who we are and we start to let go of the relationships we had built earlier in order to fit in. We ask ourselves if we can love? If we can make a long-term commitment, we are confident and happy. If we cannot form intimate relationships, we might end up feeling isolated and lonely. Our friends and partners are now center to our development.
Generatively vs. Stagnation, Adulthood (40-65)
stage 7

When we reach our forties we become comfortable, use our leisure time creatively and maybe begin contributing to society We establish our career, settle down within a relationship, begin our families and develop a sense of being part of bigger picture, we give back to society through raising our children being productive at work. Becoming involved in community activities organization, this is middle adult hood. Our concern is Generativity. If we think that we are able to lead the next generation into this world, we are happy. If we did not resolve some conflicts earlier, we can become pessimistic and experience stagnation. People at home and at work are now who influence us most..
Integrity vs. Despair , Late adulthood (65+)
stage 8

As we grow older we tend to slow down explore life as a retired person here we begin to look back over our lives. We ask: how have I done? If we think we did well, we develop feelings of contentment and integrity. Success in this stage will lead to the virtue of wisdom; wisdom enables a person to look back or their life with sense of closure and completeness and also accepts death without fear If not, we can experience despair and become grumpy and bitter. Time to compare us with mankind.
